# Design Strategy — Timothy McGuire Portfolio

## Brand Voice

**Editorial. Minimal. Explainable.**

| Pillar | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Editorial | Deliberate, curated, confident. Every word earns its place. |
| Minimal | No decoration for its own sake. Simplicity as a position of strength. |
| Explainable | Complex work made clear. Design decisions that can be articulated. |

### Tone
- Direct and confident, not boastful.
- Expert peer, not vendor.
- Warm but not casual.
- Never hedges: "I help" not "I try to help."

### What to avoid
- Corporate jargon ("synergize", "leverage", "solutions")
- Vague descriptors ("passionate", "results-driven")
- Em-dashes, ellipses used decoratively
- Exclamation points

## Content Principles

### Headlines
- Specific over generic: "AI-Native Product Design" not "Digital Design"
- Outcome-first: "Built for Results" not "High Quality Work"
- 5 words or fewer for primary headlines when possible

### Body Copy
- Lead with the benefit, not the process
- One idea per sentence
- No bullet points that start with the same word
- Max 3 bullets per section

### CTAs
- Primary: "Let's Talk" (contact page)
- Secondary: "View Work" (solutions/case studies)
- Tertiary: "Learn More" or directional arrow inline links
- Never: "Click Here", "Submit", "Learn More About Our Services"

## Design Decision Framework

When adding any new element, run through:

1. **Does it add information?** If not, remove it.
2. **Does it match the token system?** Color, type, shape.
3. **Does it work in dark mode?** Test before shipping.
4. **Does it work at mobile width?** No horizontal overflow.
5. **Is the CTA contrast AA-compliant?** White text on ink, blue on white.

## AI / Agentic Design Philosophy

Core belief: AI should make humans more capable, not replace human judgment.

Design principles for AI UX:
- **Legibility** — users must understand what the AI is doing and why
- **Control** — humans stay in the loop at critical decision points
- **Trust** — incremental transparency builds confidence over time
- **Error recovery** — failure states are first-class design problems

This philosophy should surface in copywriting, case study framing, and service descriptions.
